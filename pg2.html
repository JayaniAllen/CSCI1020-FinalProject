Details on Abstract

Abstract art uses visual language of shape, form, color and line to create a composition which may exist with a degree of independence from visual references in the world. Abstract art, non-figurative art, non-objective art, and non-representational art are all closely related terms. For more information on Abstract

The History

Abstract art in its strictest sense has its origins in the 19th century. The period characterized by so vast a body of elaborately representational art produced for the sake of illustrating anecdote also produced a number of painters who examined the mechanism of light and visual perception. All the major movements of the first two decades of the 20th century. It emphasized the gap between art and natural appearances.

The Impact

Abstract art significantly impacts viewers by encouraging personal interpretation, stimulating creativity, and allowing for a deep emotional connection through its non-representational forms, enabling people to engage with the artwork on a more introspective level and potentially access subconscious thoughts and feelings; it also played a pivotal role in the development of modern art by challenging traditional aesthetics and paving the way for new artistic explorations.

Some Comparison

Here are some things that are similar to abstract:

  1. Fauvism
    • Fauvism is a style of painting that is known for its bold colors, expressive brushstrokes, and simplified forms.
  2. Expressionism
    • A painting style that uses visible brushstrokes to celebrate the medium of paint. Close to realism but celebrates the use of paint
  3. Cubism
    • Cubism is an art style that uses geometric shapes to depict three-dimensional subjects on a flat canvas.
