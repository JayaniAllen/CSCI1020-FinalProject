Details on Realism

Realism in art is a style that aims to depict the world as it is, without artificiality or supernatural elements. It's also an art movement that depicts reality in a realistic and authentic way. For more information on Realism

The History

Realism was an artistic movement that emerged in France in the 1840s, around the 1848 Revolution.[1] Realists rejected Romanticism, which had dominated French literature and art since the early 19th century. Realism revolted against the exotic subject matter and the exaggerated emotionalism and drama of the Romantic movement. Instead, it sought to portray real and typical contemporary people and situations with truth and accuracy. It did not avoid unpleasant or sordid aspects of life.The first realism artists was Gustave Courbet known as the the "father of European realism". The term "realism" was coined by the French novelist Champfleury in the 1840s.

The technological and scientific achievements of the time influenced the Realist movement. Realism today is considered as one of the most important art movements of the 19th century.

The Impact

Realism brought a shift in art and literature by focusing on depicting everyday life and ordinary people with accuracy, often addressing social issues, and rejecting idealized portrayals, making art more relatable and accessible to the viewer while simultaneously providing a critical commentary on society through truthful representations of the world around them.

Some Comparison

Here are some things that are similar to realism:

  1. Naturalism
    • The style of creating realistic works that closely resemble the real world. Similar to realism in its goal of accurately depicting the real world.
  2. Painterly
    • A painting style that uses visible brushstrokes to celebrate the medium of paint. Close to realism but celebrates the use of paint
  3. Impressionism
    • A painting style that emphasizes on light and color, and its depiction of modern life. It is an evolution of realism.

Why is it Important?

Realism gained momentum after the French Revolution of 1848. It was aimed at rejecting the values of Romanticism and history painting. Artists like Gustave Courbet turned to a more realistic approach to art that involved depicting day-to-day lives. Realists focused on the present time, human experience, and everyday people in ordinary settings, which were previously considered unworthy of depiction in art. Through realist paintings, artists offered the public a different perspective on French society and urged people to start acknowledging the unpleasant aspects of life.

Realism changed traditional art forms, depicting the lives of ordinary people, and helping to establish modern art.

Realism is important in art, literature, and international relations because it offers an authentic depiction of reality, a new way of writing, and a framework for understanding the world.

How to Perform Realism

To draw realistically, focus on accurately capturing the form and details of your subject by utilizing techniques like proper shading, understanding light and shadow, paying close attention to perspective, and meticulously rendering textures, all while carefully observing your reference image to translate the three-dimensional quality of an object onto your paper.
